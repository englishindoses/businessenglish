Grammar Review

Four Key Tenses

A quick review of four tenses you use every day when talking about work, daily life and travel.

1. Present Simple — habits, routines and general facts.

  • I usually finish work at 6 pm.
  • She travels to London for business twice a year.
  • We don't work on weekends.

2. Past Simple — completed actions at a specific time in the past.

  • I visited Paris for a conference last month.
  • They didn't take any holiday last year.
  • We went to the beach every weekend last summer.

3. Present Perfect — life experiences and situations connected to now.

  • I've worked here for five years.
  • She has never been to Japan.
  • We've just come back from a two-week vacation.

4. Future with "going to" — plans and intentions.

  • I'm going to take a week off in August.
  • They're going to visit their family this Christmas.
  • She isn't going to work overtime this weekend.
