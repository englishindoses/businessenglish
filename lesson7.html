Grammar Review

Reported Speech for Summarising

When you summarise what others have said in a meeting, you'll almost always use reported speech. The key rule is the tense backshift: when the reporting verb is in the past (said, mentioned, explained), the verb that follows usually moves one step back in time.

1. Present simple → Past simple

  • "I need more time." → Ana said she needed more time.
  • "We don't have the budget." → Pedro mentioned that they didn't have the budget.
  • "The client wants a demo." → Marina explained that the client wanted a demo.

2. Present continuous → Past continuous

  • "We are working on it." → João said they were working on it.
  • "I am waiting for approval." → Ana mentioned that she was waiting for approval.
  • "The team is struggling with the deadline." → He pointed out that the team was struggling with the deadline.

3. Present perfect / Past simple → Past perfect

  • "We have finished phase one." → Marina explained that they had finished phase one.
  • "I spoke to the supplier." → Pedro said he had spoken to the supplier.
  • "We haven't received the feedback yet." → She mentioned that they hadn't received the feedback yet.

4. Will → Would

  • "I will send the figures on Friday." → Pedro said he would send the figures on Friday.
  • "We will review it next week." → Ana mentioned that they would review it next week.
  • "The new policy will take effect in June." → He explained that the new policy would take effect in June.

💡 Professional Reporting Verbs

Instead of just "said", try: mentioned that, pointed out that, explained that, raised the point that, made the point that.

📖 Meeting in Action

A Messy Meeting

Read this short extract from a meeting. It's messy — just like real life.

Carlos: ...so basically the delivery date is going to slip by at least two weeks, maybe three.

Rita: Sorry, can I just jump in? When you say "slip", do you mean the whole project or just phase two?

Carlos: Good question — just phase two. The vendor hasn't confirmed the equipment yet.

Rita: Right. So what I'm hearing is that phase one is still on track but phase two depends on the vendor. Is that fair?

Carlos: Yeah, exactly. And honestly, I think we should also talk about—

Luís: Sorry to interrupt — can we just stay on the delivery for a sec? I want to make sure we've got a plan before we move on.

Carlos: Sure, sure. So what I was trying to say is — if the vendor confirms by Friday, we can still hit the original date. If not, we'll need to push it back.

Luís: OK. So just to recap — Carlos, you said the delay is only on phase two, and it depends on the vendor confirming by Friday. Rita, you mentioned that phase one is still on track. Are we all on the same page?

Listen and Summarise

📊

The Budget Meeting

(For the teacher to read aloud)

"Right, so the main issue is that we've already gone over budget on the Henderson project. I spoke to finance this morning and they said we need to cut costs by fifteen percent before the end of the quarter. I think we should start by reviewing the external consultants — we're paying for three and I'm not sure we need all of them. Also, I'll set up a meeting with the client next week to renegotiate the scope."

🏢

The Office Move

(For the teacher to read aloud)

"So, quick update on the office move. We've confirmed the new building — it's the one on Rua Augusta. We're moving on the first weekend of next month. IT is handling the server migration on Friday evening, and everyone needs to pack their desks by Thursday. I'm not happy about the parking situation though — there are only forty spaces for eighty staff. I've raised it with management but they haven't got back to me yet."

🐕

The Dog Walker's Dilemma

(For the teacher to read aloud)

"OK so basically I walk six dogs every morning and it was going fine until last Tuesday. Max — the big one, the German Shepherd — he saw a cat and just went for it. The lead snapped and he dragged the poodle into a puddle. The poodle's owner is furious and says she won't pay until I buy a new coat for Biscuit. Meanwhile, I've told her that Biscuit actually seemed to enjoy the puddle. Anyway, I'm thinking of introducing a 'no cats' route."

💡 Tip

Don't try to repeat everything word for word — summarise the key points. For example: "She mentioned that they had already gone over budget. She explained that finance had said they needed to cut costs by fifteen percent..."
